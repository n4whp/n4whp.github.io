Patron Saint of Amateur Radio

St. Maximilian Kolbe, pray for us.

St. Maximilian Kolbe was a Polish Franciscan friar, priest, and martyr, known for his deep devotion to the Virgin Mary and his courageous act of self-sacrifice during World War II. Born on January 8, 1894, he founded the Militia Immaculatae (Army of the Immaculate One) and was a prolific communicator, using print and radio to spread Christian teachings. Kolbe is remembered for his ultimate act of love when he voluntarily took the place of a fellow prisoner who was condemned to death by starvation in the Auschwitz concentration camp. He died on August 14, 1941, and was canonized by Pope John Paul II in 1982.

St. Maximilian Kolbe's dedication to communication and his use of media made him a fitting Patron Saint of amateur radio operators. He established a radio station at his monastery in Poland (call sign SP3RN) and was passionate about using modern technology to share the message of faith. His pioneering spirit in the field of communication and his willingness to offer his life for another are inspirations to many. In 1984, the International Catholic Association of Radio Amateurs (ICARA) named him their patron saint, recognizing his contributions to media and his life of selfless service.
